2 · Flight data fields

FieldMeaning
DLH1913Callsign — the flight's radio identity. First 3 letters = airline ICAO code (DLH = Lufthansa, AUA = Austrian, RYR = Ryanair), rest = flight number/suffix. Private aircraft show their registration (D-EABC, OE-KTM, N123AB).
OLB > MUCRoute — origin > destination as IATA airport codes (OLB = Olbia, MUC = Munich, JFK = New York). Looked up from the callsign; "route unknown" is normal for private, military, and some charter flights.
DISTDistance from your home position to the aircraft, in km (ground distance, not slant range).
BRGBearing — the compass direction from YOU to the aircraft, in degrees (0=N, 90=E, 180=S, 270=W) plus an 8-way compass word. "BRG 126 SE" = look southeast.
ALTAltitude in metres (converted from the barometric altitude broadcast in feet). Cruise is ~10-12 km; on final approach into MUC you'll see a few hundred metres.
V/SVertical speed in m/s. Positive = climbing (shown bright), negative = descending (amber). ±0 = level flight. A typical descent is −3 to −8 m/s.
SPDGround speed in km/h (converted from knots). Jets cruise ~800-900 km/h, fly approach at ~250-300 km/h; small props ~150-250 km/h.
TRKTrack — the direction the aircraft is actually moving over the ground (not where its nose points). The aircraft symbol and its path stub point this way.
SQKSquawk — the 4-digit transponder code (section 3).
A/CAircraft type + registration — ICAO type designator (section 5) and the tail number.
CPAClosest Point of Approach — prediction of how close the aircraft will get to you and when, computed from its current track and speed. "CPA 3.1km in 1m16s" = in 76 seconds it will be 3.1 km away, then recede. "away now" = already past its closest point.
in 4 minETA to MUC — distance to the airport divided by current ground speed. An estimate, not the official landing time (no holding patterns included!).
LOOK SEShortcut of BRG: which way to turn your head to see the aircraft out the window.

3 · Squawk codes

The squawk is a 4-digit octal code (0000–7777) that air traffic control assigns to each flight so it shows uniquely on their radar. Most codes are arbitrary ATC assignments — but a few have fixed, universal meanings:

CodeMeaning
7700General emergency — engine trouble, medical emergency, anything requiring priority. The radar jumps to this aircraft immediately and marks it with the red "!" dot.
7600Radio failure — the aircraft can't communicate with ATC.
7500Unlawful interference (hijacking). Extremely rare.
7000VFR conspicuity (Europe) — small aircraft flying visually without an assigned code. You'll see this on most Cessnas and gliders around Freising.
2000Default code when entering airspace without an assignment (common on IFR flights before ATC assigns one).
1000IFR flight where the Mode-S transponder ID is used instead of a discrete code — very common around Munich.
0020/0023...Codes in the 00xx block are often rescue helicopters and police in Germany (e.g. 0020 = rescue helicopter / SAR).
If you ever see 7500/7600/7700 on the device, it's real: the aircraft's crew set it deliberately. 7700 near MUC usually means a priority landing — watch the arrival flow change around it.

4 · Weather (METAR) decoder

The WEATHER page decodes the official METAR for EDDM (Munich). A METAR is a coded hourly/half-hourly airport observation. Example:

METAR EDDM 021620Z AUTO 26010KT CAVOK 26/12 Q1020

TokenMeaning
EDDMStation: Munich Airport (ICAO code; MUC is the IATA code).
021620ZDay 02 of the month, 16:20 UTC ("Zulu" time).
26010KTWind: FROM 260° (west-southwest) at 10 knots (~19 km/h). "G24" = gusts to 24 kt. "VRB03KT" = variable direction, 3 kt.
9999 / 4000Visibility in metres; 9999 means 10 km or more.
CAVOK"Ceiling And Visibility OK" — visibility ≥10 km, no cloud below 5000 ft, no significant weather. The nicest word in aviation.
FEW040
SCT025
BKN018
OVC004		Cloud layers: coverage + height in hundreds of feet.
FEW = 1-2/8 of the sky, SCT (scattered) = 3-4/8, BKN (broken) = 5-7/8, OVC (overcast) = 8/8.
BKN018 = broken layer at 1800 ft. The lowest BKN/OVC layer is the ceiling. NSC/NCD = no significant cloud.
26/12Temperature / dew point in °C. M05 = −5 °C. Close together = fog risk.
Q1020QNH — sea-level air pressure in hPa; pilots set their altimeters to it. Standard is 1013.
RA SN FG BR
DZ TS SH GR HZ		Weather: RA rain, SN snow, FG fog, BR mist, DZ drizzle, TS thunderstorm, SH showers, GR hail, HZ haze. Prefix "−" light, "+" heavy: −RA = light rain, +TSRA = heavy thunderstorm with rain.

Field status (bottom pill)

StatusRule (from visibility + ceiling)
NORMAL OPSVisibility ≥ 8 km AND ceiling ≥ 3000 ft (or no ceiling) — VFR-like conditions, normal flow.
MARGINAL OPSVisibility ≥ 3 km AND ceiling ≥ 1000 ft — expect instrument approaches, possible small delays.
LOW VIS OPSBelow that — low-visibility procedures: bigger spacing between arrivals, delays likely.

Runway in use (RWY row)

Aircraft take off and land into the wind. Munich's parallel runways point 082°/262°, so wind from the west (typical) → 26 L/R operations (landings approach from the east); wind from the east → 08 L/R. The device estimates this from the METAR wind direction; "VRB" or calm wind gives no answer.
