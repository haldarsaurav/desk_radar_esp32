ESP32‑C3 Super Mini

board marking HW‑466AB · USB‑C · onboard PCB antenna
Wi‑Fi + BLE SoCRISC‑Vsingle‑core · 160 MHz

A thumb‑sized development board built around Espressif's ESP32‑C3 — a 32‑bit RISC‑V microcontroller with 2.4 GHz Wi‑Fi and Bluetooth 5 Low Energy on a single die. In this project it is the whole computer: it joins your Wi‑Fi, pulls live flight and weather data, and drives the round display over SPI. Powered and flashed through one USB‑C port.

Onboard controls & notes

FeatureDetail
BOOT buttonOn GPIO9. Reused as the page button in this project. Held during power‑up it forces the ROM bootloader — handy for flashing, annoying if pressed by accident (just re‑plug).
RST buttonHardware reset. BOOT + RST is the manual flashing handshake.
User LEDOnboard LED on GPIO8 (also a strapping pin — leave floating at boot).
Strapping pinsGPIO2, GPIO8, GPIO9 influence boot mode; fine for normal use once running.

As wired in the MUC Desk Radar
