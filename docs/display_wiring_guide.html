🛩️ Plane Radar — Display Wiring Guide

GC9A01 1.28″ round display  ⟶  ESP32-C3 Super Mini  •  7–8 wires total  •  USB must be unplugged while wiring
⚠️ The one fatal mistake: display VCC goes to 3.3V (pin labelled "3V3") — NEVER to the 5V pin. 5V destroys the display.

1 · The wires — match the label on the display to the label printed on the ESP32

Your display's pins are labelled on its little connector strip. The ESP32 Super Mini has tiny white labels printed next to each pin hole ("5V", "GND", "3V3", "0", "1", "2", "3", "4"… — "4" means GPIO4). Just connect label to label:

1
VCC display power
3V3 the 3.3-volt pin — NOT 5V!
2
GND ground
GND any GND pin
3
SCL also printed SCK or CLK
4 GPIO4
4
SDA also printed MOSI or DIN
3 GPIO3
5
DC data / command
10 GPIO10 — opposite side of board
6
CS chip select
1 GPIO1
7
RST also printed RES
0 GPIO0
8
BLK backlight — only if present
3V3 same pin as wire 1 (or leave unconnected — many modules default to ON)

3 · Order of work

  1. Unplug USB from the ESP32.
  2. Connect the 7 (or 8) wires above. Female-female jumper wires work if both sides have header pins.
  3. Double-check VCC → 3V3 one more time.
  4. Plug USB back in. The board's blue LED should blink like before — if anything gets hot or the LED stays dark, unplug immediately.
  5. Tell Claude — the display test sketch gets flashed next, and the screen should light up.
✔ Nothing on the screen after flashing the test sketch? That's almost always SCL/SDA swapped, or a loose jumper. Don't panic — it's reversible, unlike the 5V mistake.
